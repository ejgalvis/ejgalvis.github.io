A little about me:

I'm a cultural analytics researcher and library worker interested in information access, media and culture studies, and critical approaches to data management and programming. My current research focuses on computational analysis of book bans in the United States as a particular node threatening information access and resource equity today.

My professional experience includes library digitization and data management, event planning and project management, and administrative work in the United States and Thailand. Previous positions took place in a combination of local, national, and regional non-profit settings as well as research universities.

In addition to my computational training from an MA in Digital Texts and Culture at the University of Chicago, I studied Classics and Gender and Sexuality Studies at Princeton University. Previous research examined aberrant birth and creation myths in Archaic Greek cosmogonies and their later reception in classical Athens and the modern period. I am particularly interested in the intersections of horror, monstrosity, and queer theory given their influence on political and cultural imaginations today.
